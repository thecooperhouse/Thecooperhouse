Notes & assumptions

  • Assumes you are an Australian resident for tax purposes, employed in Australia, with no other income, deductions, dependants beyond what's stated above, or reportable fringe benefits. Non-residents and working holiday makers are taxed differently.
  • PAYG figures are calculated by annualising your income, applying the full-year formula, then dividing back down. This closely matches employer withholding but won't be cent-for-cent identical to the ATO's official per-period withholding schedules (NAT 1004/1006 etc.), which use their own rounding rules — use the ATO's withholding calculator if you need an exact payroll figure.
  • The Medicare Levy Surcharge here uses individual/family income thresholds only — it doesn't account for dependent children (which raise the family threshold) or other adjustments to "income for MLS purposes" (e.g. reportable fringe benefits, super contributions).
  • HELP/HECS repayments for 2025–26 and 2026–27 use the ATO's current marginal repayment system. Earlier years use the pre-reform flat-rate schedule, indexed proportionally from the published 2024–25 table — treat these as indicative only.
  • 2026–27 Medicare Levy Surcharge and low-income threshold figures are carried over from 2025–26 pending the ATO's official indexed figures. 2027–28 income tax rates are legislated; HELP thresholds for that year are estimated from historical indexation trends, not yet officially published.
  • Other income (bonuses, interest, dividends) is assessed annually at your marginal rate. Franked dividends are grossed up using the 30% company tax rate — appropriate for dividends from large listed companies; base-rate entities frank at 25%, which this tool doesn't model. Franking credits are treated as fully refundable, per current law. Note that in practice, tax on bonuses is withheld by your employer when paid, while tax on interest and dividends is settled at return time — the per-pay-cycle table simply spreads everything evenly, so treat it as an average rather than what any single payslip will show.
  • Division 293 tax (the extra 15% on concessional super contributions for high income earners) is shown as a separate estimate when your income plus concessional contributions exceeds the $250,000 threshold. It assumes only employer Super Guarantee plus any voluntary salary sacrifice you enter — it doesn't account for other employers, defined benefit funds, reportable fringe benefits, or investment losses that also count towards "Division 293 income". Unlike the figures above, it's not withheld through PAYG — the ATO bills it separately after your return is assessed.
  • This tool is for general estimation only and is not tax advice. Figures are based on ATO rates believed current as of July 2026 — always confirm against ato.gov.au or a registered tax agent before making decisions.
