Can the Broncos still make the eight?

Mathematically alive · realistically almost gone

Step 1 — the ceiling. Brisbane has 18 points with 7 games left and no byes remaining. Two points per win:

18 + (7 × 2) = 32 points maximum ← requires winning all seven

Step 2 — the floor they must clear. Twelve teams sit above them. Five of those twelve must finish below 32 points (or tie on 32 with worse differential) for Brisbane to reach 8th.

Step 3 — what each rival is allowed to do. Byes are guaranteed points, so a team's "floor" is its current total plus 2 for each bye still to come. That floor determines how many of their remaining games they may win before they lock Brisbane out:

Step 4 — the verdict. Penrith cannot be caught at all: their current 32 already equals Brisbane's absolute maximum, and Brisbane's differential of −124 loses every tiebreak. So Brisbane is chasing eight places among the remaining eleven, needing five of them to collapse.

Brisbane wins 7/7 → 32 pts Warriors & Roosters win 0/7 → 30 pts each Sharks / Dolphins / Knights ≤1 → 30 pts each … and Brisbane's −124 differential still loses any tie on 32

Put plainly: Brisbane must win every remaining game — including away trips to the Cowboys, Dolphins, Raiders and Bulldogs, and home games against the Knights, Warriors and Storm — while the Warriors and Roosters lose all seven of theirs and three more contenders win no more than one apiece. Each of those is unlikely on its own; together they compound into something close to zero. The elimination is not yet formal, but any single loss from here makes it formal immediately.

The one caveat: Brisbane's terrible differential (−124) means ties don't help them. Even reaching 32 points and drawing level with a rival almost certainly still leaves them outside the eight. In practice they need those five teams to finish clearly below 32, not merely level with it.

Looking to 2026/27

  • The Championship expands its play-offs from four teams to six this season — a meaningfully wider door for a club that finished 17th.
  • A new club crest debuts, reviving the 1977–92 badge: bottle kiln, Staffordshire knot, red-and-white stripes.
  • Stuart Harvey appointed technical director in May, joining sporting director Jon Walters in a restructured recruitment setup.
  • Mark Robins stays on for his second full season, backed by owner John Coates with real money — Stoke have been among the Championship's bigger spenders this window.
  • For Brisbane viewing: a 3pm Saturday kick-off in Stoke lands around midnight AEST.
