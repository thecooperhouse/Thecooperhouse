How to read the timing data: the gold chip next to the winner is their total race time; the chips next to P2 and P3 are how far behind they finished (≈ marks a figure reported to the nearest tenth or as "around X seconds" rather than official timing). "FL" is the fastest lap of that race and who set it — the driver/rider who set it wasn't always on the podium.

Circuit records: for F1 the "circuit best" is the official race lap record — the fastest lap ever set during a race on the current layout (F1's 2026-spec cars are around 2s/lap slower, so most records date from the 2018–2025 era). For MotoGP it's the all-time circuit record, which in bike racing usually falls in qualifying; where the record fell during a 2026 weekend the card says so.

Data snapshot compiled 17 July 2026 from official series results, timing reports and press coverage. Race fastest-lap times are shown for every F1 Grand Prix and for every MotoGP Grand Prix except Hungary, Assen and Sachsenring, where the official time wasn’t published in a citable source at time of writing — the fastest-lap data for those lives on the official timing sheets and can be added on the next refresh. Sprint fastest laps are rarely published; Catalunya’s is shown. Per-podium-finisher individual fastest laps also come from the full FIA/FIM timing sheets — where a podium finisher set the race’s overall fastest lap you’ll see it against their name. Ask Claude for a refreshed version after future rounds; the spoiler shield moves to the newest race automatically.
